# OHC Foundation Website Strategy

## Core Positioning

Open Healthcare Network Foundation stewards open, standards-based digital public infrastructure for healthcare. It maintains CARE and related open-source building blocks that help governments, hospitals, NGOs, and implementation partners deploy interoperable, self-hostable, AI-ready health systems without vendor lock-in.

CARE is the flagship platform and proof point. The foundation is the neutral institutional steward of the wider open healthcare commons.

## Homepage Narrative

1. Hero: OHC Foundation as steward of open healthcare DPI.
2. Trust strip: verified DPG, MIT license, standards-first architecture, public deployment history.
3. Problem: health systems repeatedly rebuild fragmented, locked-in infrastructure.
4. Foundation answer: governance, security, releases, documentation, implementation playbooks, and neutral stewardship.
5. CARE platform: CARE Core plus Apps for real healthcare workflows.
6. Standards: FHIR R5, SNOMED CT, LOINC, UCUM, ICD-10, ABDM, open APIs, terminology services.
7. Impact: case-study led, with metrics moved to a verification register.
8. AI: assistive, human-in-the-loop, workflow-aware, and grounded in structured data.
9. Ecosystem: audience journeys for governments, funders, hospitals, SIs, developers, and clinicians.
10. Support: funding the commons, not one-off deployment work.

## Audience Journeys

- Governments: sovereignty, local ownership, standards, scale, procurement confidence.
- Funders: public-good leverage, governance, security, documentation, reuse, sustainability.
- Hospitals: HMIS/EMR workflows, reliability, labs, pharmacy, billing, reporting, staff adoption.
- Implementation partners: deployment opportunity, plugin model, contribution rules, release compatibility.
- Developers: open-source contribution, Django DRF, React TypeScript, real deployment context.
- Clinicians: workflow review, clinical safety, terminology, forms, documentation burden, AI review.
- Global digital health institutions: standards-first architecture, country-led reuse, open governance.

## Metrics Policy

Impact claims should be published only when each metric has:

- Program
- Value
- As-of date
- Source
- Public/private status
- Verifying owner
- Safe-to-publish status

The site should not mix old COVID-era numbers, current HMIS metrics, TeleICU scale, palliative care numbers, contributor counts, and internal deployment updates in a single headline block.

## Content To Avoid

- SaaS/vendor positioning
- Charity-style donation framing
- AI autonomy or diagnosis claims
- Unapproved partner, donor, hospital, or MoU details
- Undated or inconsistent deployment metrics
- "United Nations recognized" phrasing unless sourced precisely through the Digital Public Goods registry
- Claims such as lives saved, national coverage, or growth percentages without verification

## Final Homepage Copy Direction

Use "Open digital public infrastructure for healthcare" as the primary headline. Explain OHC Foundation first, then CARE. Make the foundation's role legible: neutral stewardship, governance, security, release discipline, documentation, standards alignment, implementation ecosystem, and long-term sustainability.
